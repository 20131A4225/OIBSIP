Stephen William Hawking

1942-2018

"Work gives you meaning and purpose and life is empty without it."

Biography

Stephen William Hawking was a renowned British theoretical physicist, cosmologist and author. He was born in Oxford, England in 1942 and was famously known for his work on black holes and relativity. He was the Dennis Stanton Avery and Sally Tsui Wong-Avery Director of Research at the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics at the University of Cambridge. His book, A Brief History of Time, is one of the most successful science books of all time. Hawking was the recipient of numerous awards and honors, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States. He died in 2018 at the age of 76.
